# [[Day 2 - Ecological Succession|Day 2 - Ecological Succession]]

Before any definition, here's a story:

> Imagine a massive volcanic eruption. Lava covers everything — no soil, no plants, no animals. Just bare rock. Now fast-forward 500 years. That same place is a dense forest full of trees, birds, insects, and mammals.
> 
> **How did life return to bare rock? That step-by-step process of one community replacing another over time is called Ecological Succession.**

---

## What is Ecological Succession?

**Ecological Succession** is the gradual, directional and predictable process of change in the species structure of an ecological community over time.

Three key words to remember:

- **Gradual** — happens slowly over decades/centuries
- **Directional** — always moves toward a stable endpoint
- **Predictable** — follows a defined sequence

The community at each stage is called a **Seral Community** (or Sere). The final stable stage is called the **Climax Community.**

## Types of Succession Based on Habitat

This is a favourite MCQ area. Know the names and their starting habitats:

|Name|Habitat|Pioneer species|
|---|---|---|
|**Xerosere**|Dry bare rock / land|Lichens|
|**Hydrosere**|Fresh water (pond/lake)|Phytoplankton, submerged plants|
|**Halosere**|Saline / coastal areas|Mangroves|
|**Lithosere**|Rocky surface|Lichens (same as xerosere)|
|**Psammosere**|Sand dunes|Marram grass|

**Exam tip:** Hydrosere is the most tested type after xerosere. The pond succession sequence — open water → submerged plants → floating plants → reed swamp → marsh/meadow → woodland — is directly asked.

## The Climax Community — Key Concept

The **climax community** is the final, self-sustaining, stable community that develops at the end of succession. Key features:

- Species composition stays **constant** unless disturbed
- **Maximum biodiversity and biomass** in that region
- Determined primarily by **climate** (temperature + rainfall)
- Each region has its own climax — tropical rainforest, grassland, tundra, etc.

**Monoclimax vs Polyclimax theory** — this is a direct NET question:

|Theory|Proposed by|Idea|
|---|---|---|
|**Monoclimax**|Clements (1916)|One region → one climax determined only by climate|
|**Polyclimax**|Tansley (1939)|Multiple climaxes possible depending on soil, fire, animals, humans|

Most ecologists today accept the **polyclimax** view. Tansley is also the person who gave us the term "ecosystem."

---

## Nudation, Ecesis, Reaction — The Process Terms

NET often asks about the mechanisms _within_ succession. Three terms to know:

**Nudation** — the creation of a bare area (by volcano, flood, fire, glacier). The starting point of succession.

**Ecesis** — the successful establishment of a species in a new area (germination, growth, reproduction).

**Reaction** — the way organisms modify their own environment, making it suitable for the _next_ seral community and unsuitable for themselves. This is the engine of succession.

> Example: Lichens dissolve rock, creating thin soil. This soil allows mosses to grow — but mosses then shade out the lichens. Reaction by lichens enabled moss invasion. This keeps repeating until climax.

---

## Retrogressive Succession — Going Backwards

Normal succession = **progressive** (simple → complex). Sometimes disturbance pushes an ecosystem _backwards_ to an earlier stage — this is **retrogressive succession** (also called **degradative succession**).

Causes: deforestation, overgrazing, fire, pollution, urbanisation.

**Exam tip:** Human activity is the main cause of retrogressive succession. This connects directly to environmental degradation questions.

## Summary — The 7 Facts You Must Remember

1. Succession = directional, gradual change from pioneer → climax community
2. Primary = no soil; Secondary = soil present
3. Pioneer on rock = **Lichens**; Pioneer in water = **phytoplankton**
4. Pyramid of energy = **never inverted** (from last lesson — connected!)
5. Climax: GPP = respiration, net production ≈ 0
6. Monoclimax = Clements; Polyclimax = Tansley
7. Retrogressive succession = human disturbance pushes ecosystem backwards
